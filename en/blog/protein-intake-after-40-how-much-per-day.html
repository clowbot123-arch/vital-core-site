Protein Intake After 40: How Much Per Day?

Simple daily targets for fat loss, muscle retention, and better recovery without overcomplicating your meals.

Use the quick calculator See high-protein recipe

Short answer: Most adults 40+ do better with 1.6-2.2 g protein per kilogram body weight daily, especially when the goal is fat loss with muscle retention.

Why protein matters more after 40

  • Muscle retention: Age-related muscle loss starts gradually in your 40s.
  • Recovery: Higher protein improves training recovery and consistency.
  • Appetite control: Protein-rich meals reduce cravings and late-evening snacking.

Protein calculator (practical range)

Use your current body weight:

  • Maintenance / general health: 1.4-1.6 g/kg
  • Fat loss + muscle retention: 1.6-2.0 g/kg
  • Training-focused phase: 1.8-2.2 g/kg

Example: At 80 kg body weight, a fat-loss target is roughly 128-160 g protein/day.

The easiest execution rule: 3-4 protein anchors per day

  1. Breakfast: 30-40 g
  2. Lunch: 35-45 g
  3. Dinner: 35-45 g
  4. Optional snack/shake: 20-30 g

This structure is easier than trying to “catch up” at night.

Best protein sources for adults 40+

  • Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, eggs
  • Chicken, turkey, fish, lean beef
  • Whey or plant protein powder for busy days
  • Beans + tofu combinations for mixed diets

Common mistakes that sabotage results

  • Underdosing breakfast (coffee + toast is not enough protein).
  • Relying on one giant dinner instead of distribution across the day.
  • Not planning fallback meals for workdays and travel.
