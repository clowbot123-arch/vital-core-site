How to Lose Visceral Fat After 40

A waist-first, metabolism-friendly plan you can run for 6 weeks without burnout.

Start the 6-week plan Set your protein baseline

Short answer: To lose visceral fat after 40, focus on consistent calorie control, high protein intake, strength training, daily steps, sleep quality, and stress management. Track your waist weekly—this is often a better progress marker than scale weight alone.

What is visceral fat (and why it matters after 40)?

Visceral fat sits around your organs, not just under your skin. Higher levels are associated with worse insulin sensitivity, higher cardiometabolic risk, and lower energy stability. The good news: it often responds well to consistent lifestyle habits, especially when you improve activity and sleep together.

6-week visceral fat reduction framework

  1. Protein first at each meal: aim for a daily target based on body weight (typically 1.6-2.2 g/kg target body weight).
  2. Strength train 2-4x/week: preserve or build muscle so fat loss comes from the right place.
  3. Hit 8,000-11,000 steps/day: this supports energy expenditure without crushing recovery.
  4. Prioritize sleep: 7-8 hours in bed with a consistent bedtime anchor.
  5. Control liquid calories and late snacks: fewer stealth calories, better appetite rhythm.
  6. Use post-meal walks: 10-15 minutes after lunch and dinner for glucose and appetite control.

How to measure progress the right way

  • Waist circumference: measure at navel level 1x/week, same time of day.
  • 7-day average weight: avoid reacting to single-day fluctuations.
  • Energy and cravings score: track 1-10 daily to spot behavior trends.

Simple day template (example)

  • Breakfast: high-protein meal + fiber
  • Lunch: protein + vegetables + smart carbs
  • Afternoon: 10-minute walk break
  • Dinner: protein + colorful vegetables + controlled carb portion
  • Evening: no random snacking; tea or planned protein snack only

Common mistakes that slow visceral fat loss

  • Doing only cardio and skipping strength work
  • Under-eating protein and over-snacking at night
  • Poor sleep + high caffeine late in the day
  • Changing strategy every 3-4 days instead of staying consistent for 4-6 weeks
