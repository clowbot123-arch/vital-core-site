üåä Understanding Sleep Phases

Our sleep consists of multiple cycles that repeat approximately every 90 minutes. Each cycle contains different sleep phases:

Light Sleep (N1)5-10 minFalling asleep phase, transition to sleep
Light Sleep (N2)10-25 minBody temperature drops, heart rate slows

The Perfect Sleep Cycle

An average night contains 4-6 complete sleep cycles. Deep sleep dominates the first half of the night, while REM sleep increases in the early morning hours.

üí° Key Insight

Deep sleep is particularly important for physical recovery and immune function. REM sleep is crucial for learning and emotional regulation. Both phases are equally important!

üßò Relaxation Techniques

4-7-8 Breathing Technique

This simple technique, derived from yoga, activates the parasympathetic nervous system:

  1. Breathe in through your nose for 4 seconds
  2. Hold your breath for 7 seconds
  3. Slowly exhale through your mouth for 8 seconds
  4. Repeat 3-4 times

üß¨ Scientific Evidence

A study in the Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine showed that the 4-7-8 breathing technique can reduce the time to fall asleep by an average of 50%.

Additional Techniques

  • Progressive Muscle Relaxation: Systematic tensing and relaxing of muscle groups
  • Body Scan Meditation: Mentally going through your entire body
  • Visualization: Imagining a calm, relaxing scene
