Protein timing for busy adults 40+

Simple structure beats perfection: spread protein across the day and anchor it around training.

Many people over 40 hit the gym consistently but still under-eat protein at key times. The result is slower recovery, more cravings, and weaker training progression. The fix is not complicated: distribute protein across meals and keep post-workout intake predictable.

A practical daily target

A common evidence-based range for active adults is around 1.6–2.2 g protein per kg body weight per day. You don’t need to hit the top end every day. Consistency in the middle of the range usually beats short bursts of perfection.

Meal-by-meal structure

  • Breakfast: 30–40g protein (e.g., eggs + skyr, or cottage cheese bowl)
  • Lunch: 30–45g protein (meat/fish/tofu + legumes or whole grains)
  • Dinner: 30–45g protein
  • Optional snack: 20–30g if needed to reach your target

Where supplements can help

Supplements are useful when your schedule is chaotic or appetite is low after training. In that case, a targeted amino support strategy can be practical. We break this down in our Advanced Amino Acid Complex review, including who benefits most and who should skip it.

Energy still low despite good protein?

If protein intake is solid but energy remains flat, look at sleep quality, training volume, and stress first. Then evaluate options like our detailed EndoPeak Vitality Formula guide to see whether it matches your context.

Action plan for the next 7 days

  1. Set a realistic protein target in the 1.6–2.2 g/kg range.
  2. Pre-plan one high-protein breakfast and one high-protein lunch.
  3. Track adherence, not perfection (5 of 7 good days is progress).
